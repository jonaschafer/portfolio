Contract

Use a basic consulting agreement. You can find templates online or ask Daniel for his. Key clauses:
Services: "Fractional Creative Director services as outlined in attached SOW"
Payment: "$X/month, due on 1st of month, net 15"
Term: "6 months starting [date], renewable"
Termination: "Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice"
IP: "All work product belongs to Client"
Confidentiality: Standard NDA language
Independent Contractor: "Contractor is not an employee"
Don't:
Overthink it
Use a 20-page contract
Negotiate for weeks
Ask for equity (keep it simple on your first one)


Kick off

Your first month is discovery and alignment:
Week 1: Stakeholder Interviews
Talk to CEO, Head of Product, Head of Marketing, anyone else relevant
Understand their POV on the brand
Learn what's working and what's not
Week 2: Current State Audit
Review all brand touchpoints (website, product, marketing, emails, etc.)
Document what's inconsistent, what's missing, what's broken
Take screenshots and organize in Figma or Notion
Week 3: Priorities Mapping
Based on interviews + audit, create a prioritized roadmap
What needs attention NOW vs. LATER
Get alignment with leadership
Week 4: First Deliverable
Could be: strategic direction doc, brand principles, redesign of one key surface
Something tangible they can react to




Tips

Time tracking:
Track your hours honestly (even if you go over, you'll learn)
Don't nickel-and-dime (a 15-min Slack thread isn't billable)
If you consistently go over 15 hours, raise it in month 2
Communication:
Set expectations early (response time, availability, meeting cadence)
Use async as much as possible (Loom videos, Figma comments, Google Docs)
Be clear when you're "on" vs. "off"
Scope creep:
It WILL happen on your first gig
When it does, say: "Happy to do that, but it's outside our current scope. Want to add it for next month?"
Don't be a pushover, but also don't be rigid on your first one
Managing contractors:
If you need to bring someone in, add 20% to their rate for your management
Example: Contractor costs $100/hr, you charge them $120/hr
Make it clear in the proposal: "Contractor costs billed at cost + 20% management fee"
Monthly check-ins:
End of each month: "Here's what we accomplished, here's what's next, here's where we stand on hours"
Gives them visibility, builds trust
